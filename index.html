Frequently asked questions

What is the WhatsApp Business API used for?
The WhatsApp Business API is designed to help businesses communicate with customers at scale. It enables businesses to send notifications, respond to customer inquiries, and provide support through WhatsApp, facilitating direct and secure conversations with customers.
What is the difference between the WhatsApp Business API and the WhatsApp Business App?

The WhatsApp Business API and the WhatsApp Business App are designed for different types of businesses and use cases:

WhatsApp Business API:

  1. The API is designed for businesses that need to manage customer communication at scale. With the API, you can integrate WhatsApp with your existing systems, like CRM platforms or customer support tools.
  2. One of the key benefits of the API is that it allows businesses to send bulk promotional messages (using pre-approved message templates) to customers. This makes it ideal for businesses looking to reach a large audience efficiently.
  3. The API also supports automation, advanced customer service workflows, and seamless integration with chatbots for personalized interactions.

WhatsApp Business App:

  1. The app is meant for small businesses and has limited functionality compared to the API. It allows you to manage customer communication directly from your phone or a simple web interface.
  2. The app doesn’t support sending bulk promotional messages, and its scalability is limited. You can only respond to individual customer inquiries and send messages manually, making it less suitable for businesses with a large customer base or high messaging volumes.
How can my business use the WhatsApp Business API?
To use the WhatsApp Business API, businesses need to apply through a WhatsApp Business Solution Provider like DotPe. After approval, your business can integrate the API with customer support platforms, CRM systems, or chatbots to communicate with customers.
What are the pricing and costs associated with the WhatsApp Business API?
Pricing for the WhatsApp Business API is based on a conversation model. You are charged per 24-hour conversation session with a customer, whether initiated by the business or the user. Charges may vary depending on the region and the type of conversation.
Can I use my existing WhatsApp number for the WhatsApp Business API?
Yes, you can use your existing WhatsApp number with the WhatsApp Business API, but it must be a phone number that is not currently in use with the WhatsApp Business App or WhatsApp Messenger. Once registered with the API, the number will be associated with the API and cannot be used with the app.
What types of messages can I send using the WhatsApp Business API?

The WhatsApp Business API allows businesses to send two types of messages: Marketing Messages and Utility Messages. Each type serves a distinct purpose and has specific rules for use:

  1. Marketing Messages:
    These messages are designed to promote your products, services, or brand. They may include special offers, new product announcements, or promotional campaigns. Marketing messages must be sent using pre-approved message templates
  2. Utility Messages:
    Utility messages are used to provide transactional or service-related information. These include updates such as order confirmations, shipping alerts, appointment reminders, or payment receipts. Utility messages ensure that important non-promotional information is communicated to the customer in a timely manner.
